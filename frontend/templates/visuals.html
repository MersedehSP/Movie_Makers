Correlation between IMDB rating, budget and duration:

Our goal was to create a ML application to indicate whether a movie will have a high enough IMDB rating to be considered "successful". We decided to use IMDB data from Kaggle, and first looked at the variables that may correlate with the rating. Both the budget and duration are quantitative fields that have a slight positive correlation with the overall IMDN rating.

We decided to bin the budget and duration according to ranges that are both easily understood by the users of our tool, as well as capturing the full range of our data. For the definition of success, we tested ratings of 6,7 and 8 as cut-offs for success. Defining it as 8 or more gave us the highest testing data score (0.89).

We discovered that our data has more unsuccessful movies, and to account for this, we balanced our model.

Analysis of Genres Over Time:

Comedy, drama and action comprise most of the movies made overall. Movie ratings have decreased over time in general, but horror movies seem to be the least successful in the eyes of IMDB raters.

Good Movies vs. Bad Movies and the Genre Breakdown:

Among the movies with ratings of 8 or higher, drama stands out as the most prevalent genre. In conclusion, many comedies are flops, and dramas are more likely to do well with the audience who submits ratings on IMDB.
